## 2. Qualification Questions (Pre-Onboarding)

Ask these before scheduling the onboarding call:

### Must-Have Info

1. **What tools do you currently use for bookings/invoices?**
   - Looking for: Bookbee, Google Forms, DMs only, notebook
   - Red flag: Already on CaterZen or enterprise software

2. **How many events do you do per month?**
   - Sweet spot: 5-25 events/month
   - Too few: May not see value; Too many: May need enterprise features

3. **Do you currently collect deposits?**
   - If yes: Great, we make it easier
   - If no: We'll need to coach them on why deposits matter

4. **How do customers usually reach out?**
   - Looking for: Instagram DMs, texts, phone calls, mix
   - This tells us how much chaos we're solving

5. **Are you a solo operator or do you have staff?**
   - Solo: Perfect for MVP
   - Staff: Time tracking comes Phase 2

### Nice-to-Have Info

6. **What's your average event size and price?**
   - Helps us understand their business scale

7. **What's your biggest frustration right now?**
   - Use their words in the demo

8. **Do you use Stripe, Venmo, Zelle, or something else for payments?**
   - Determines if we demo Stripe path or manual path

## 3. Onboarding Call Agenda (30 Minutes)

### Before the Call

- [ ] Review their Instagram - note their food style, recent events
- [ ] Have CaterFlow open in a browser
- [ ] Have their intake link ready: `caterflow.app/book/heavenly-kitchen`
- [ ] Prepare to share screen

### Call Structure

**Minutes 0-5: Listen**

> "Before I show you anything, walk me through what happens when someone DMs you asking about catering. Start from the DM and go all the way to getting paid."

Take notes. Identify:
- Where they lose time
- Where they lose leads
- What they hate doing

Reflect back: "So you're spending X time on Y, and you're losing leads because Z. Is that right?"

**Minutes 5-10: Show the Intake Form**

> "Let me show you what your customers will see."

- Share screen
- Open the intake form: `caterflow.app/book/heavenly-kitchen`
- Walk through each step:
  - "They enter their contact info..."
  - "Then the event details - date, time, guests, location..."
  - "Then they tell you dietary needs, budget..."
  - "And how they heard about you - so you know what marketing is working"
- Emphasize: "When they submit, you get everything you need to quote. No back-and-forth."

> "Does this capture everything you'd need to write a quote?"

**Minutes 10-20: Build a Quote Together**

> "Now let me show you what you see when an inquiry comes in."

- Show the dashboard
- Click into a sample inquiry
- Click "Create Quote"
- Ask: "What's your most popular menu for a 30-person event? Let's build it together."

**Build the quote live:**
- Add their actual menu items (get names and prices from them)
- Add delivery/setup fee
- Set deposit to 30%
- Show the preview

> "This is what your client will see. They can accept and pay the deposit right here."

**Minutes 20-25: Enter Their Top 5 Clients**

> "Let me enter your most important clients right now so you can see how the CRM works."

Ask: "Give me the names of your 5 best customers. The ones who order multiple times a year."

Enter each one:
- Name
- Phone or email
- Any notes they want to add

> "Now when Marcus texts you 'same as last time,' you search his name and see everything in 2 seconds."

**Minutes 25-30: Set Up Their Booking Link**

> "Last thing - let's put this link where your customers can find it."

Help them update their Instagram bio:
- Copy the link: `caterflow.app/book/heavenly-kitchen`
- Open Instagram on their phone
- Add to bio

> "From now on, when someone DMs asking about catering, you reply: 'Here's the link to request a quote: [link]. Fill this out and I'll get back to you within 24 hours.'"

### After the Call

Send a follow-up text within 1 hour:

The one thing to do today: put that link in your Instagram bio. That's it.

## 5. Objection Handling

### "I'm not tech-savvy"
> "That's exactly who this is for. If you can text, you can use this. I'll walk you through everything, and you can text me anytime you're stuck."

### "What if my clients don't want to fill out a form?"
> "Some won't - and that's okay. For those, you can create an inquiry manually in 30 seconds. But most customers actually prefer it because it's faster than going back and forth over DMs."

### "I don't want to pay for another tool"
> "Totally fair. That's why you get 3 months free. If you're not saving time and making more money by then, don't pay. But most caterers tell me they stop opening Bookbee within 2 weeks."

### "I'm worried about switching / what if I lose data?"
> "You can run both tools for the first month. Nothing changes until you're confident. And you can export all your data from CaterFlow anytime - it's your data."

### "Can I think about it?"
> "Of course. What's your biggest hesitation? I want to make sure I'm addressing it."
